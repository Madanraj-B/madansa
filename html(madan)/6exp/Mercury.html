Mercury

  1. Mercury is a chemical element with the symbol Hg and atomic number 80. It is a heavy, silvery-white liquid metal at room temperature and is the only metal that is liquid under standard conditions. Here’s an overview of key aspects of mercury:
  2. Physical and Chemical Properties:
    • Appearance: Mercury is a shiny, metallic liquid with a silvery appearance. It forms droplets at room temperature.
    • Density: It is dense, about 13.6 times denser than water.
    • Melting Point: Mercury has a low melting point of −38.83°C (−37.89°F).
    • Boiling Point: Its boiling point is 356.7°C (674°F)
    • Conductivity: It is a good conductor of electricity and heat, though not as efficient as other metals like copper.
    • Toxicity: Mercury is highly toxic, especially in its vapor form or as methylmercury (a more toxic organic compound), which can accumulate in living organisms.
  3. Occurrence and Sources:
    • Natural Occurrence: Mercury is primarily found in the mineral cinnabar (mercury sulfide, HgS), which is the main ore from which mercury is extracted.
    • Distribution: It occurs in trace amounts in the Earth's crust, and small amounts can be found in rocks, soil, and water.
    • Man-made Sources: Mercury can also be released into the environment from industrial activities such as coal burning, mining, and the production of chlorine and caustic soda
  4. Historical Use:
    • Mercury has been known since ancient times and was used by the ancient Egyptians and Romans.
    • It has been used in thermometers, barometers, and various other scientific instruments.
    • Historically, mercury was also used in gold mining (amalgamation) and in some medicines (though this is now discouraged due to its toxicity).
