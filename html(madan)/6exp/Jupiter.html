Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System, known for its massive size, strong magnetic field, and distinctive bands of clouds. Here are some key facts about it:

  1. Size and Composition
    • Jupiter is a gas giant, made primarily of hydrogen and helium.
    • It has a diameter of about 139,820 kilometers (86,881 miles) — roughly 11 times that of Earth.
    • Jupiter’s mass is about 318 times that of Earth, and its volume could contain over 1,300 Earths.
  2. Atmosphere
    • Jupiter's atmosphere is characterized by bands of clouds at different altitudes, including the famous Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has been raging for at least 400 years.
    • The atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, water vapor, and ammonia.
  3. Moons
    • Jupiter has at least 95 known moons, including Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. These four were discovered by Galileo in 1610 and are some of the largest moons in the Solar System.
    • Europa is particularly interesting for its subsurface ocean, which may have conditions suitable for life.
  4. Magnetic Field
    • Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet in the Solar System, 14 times stronger than Earth's. This magnetic field generates powerful radiation belts around the planet.
  5. Rings
    • Jupiter has a faint ring system, discovered in 1979 by the Voyager spacecraft. These rings are made primarily of dust particles and are very difficult to see.
