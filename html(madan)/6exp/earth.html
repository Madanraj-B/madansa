Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet in the universe that supports life. Here are some key details about Earth:

  1. Basic Facts
    • Distance from the Sun: About 93 million miles (150 million kilometers).
    • Diameter: Approximately 7,917.5 miles (12,742 kilometers) at the equator.
    • Circumference: Roughly 24,901 miles (40,075 kilometers).
    • Surface Area: About 196.9 million square miles (510.1 million square kilometers).
    • Mass: 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg.
    • Average Temperature: Varies widely, but the global average is around 59°F (15°C).
  2. Composition and Structure
    • Crust: The outermost solid layer where we live. It's made of rock and is divided into tectonic plates.
    • Mantle: Beneath the crust, made of semi-solid rock, which flows slowly over time. It's responsible for Earth's internal heat
    • Outer Core: A layer of liquid iron and nickel, which generates Earth's magnetic field.
    • Inner Core: A solid ball of iron and nickel, the hottest part of Earth, with temperatures reaching up to 9,000°F (5,000°C).
  3. Atmosphere
    • Nitrogen: 78%
    • Oxygen: 21%
    • Argon: 0.93%
    • Carbon Dioxide: 0.04% (but increasing due to human activities)
    • Other gases: Including neon, helium, and methane
  4. Water and Oceans
    • Earth's surface is about 71% water, with the vast majority being ocean (around 96.5%).
    • The remaining 3.5% is fresh water, found mostly in glaciers, ice caps, and underground reservoirs.
